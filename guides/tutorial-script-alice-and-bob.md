# Zeyaroo Tutorial — "Alice & Bob" Role-Play Script

**Format:** Two-narrator screen-record tutorial. Alice (creator) on the left half of the screen, Bob (client) on the right. A neutral **Narrator** voice ties scenes together.

**Scenario:** Bob is getting married and wants to hire Alice, a wedding photographer he found on Instagram, for his wedding shoot.

---

## Cast

- **Alice Perera** — wedding photographer. Has a Zeyaroo creator account.
- **Bob Fernando** — about to get married. New to Zeyaroo.
- **Narrator** — voice-over guide.

---

## SCENE 1 — Discovery & Inquiry

**[ON SCREEN: Bob's browser, Alice's public creator profile on Zeyaroo]**

**NARRATOR:** Every collaboration on Zeyaroo starts the same way — a client finds a creator, and sends an inquiry. No phone numbers exchanged, no DMs lost in Instagram. Everything stays in one thread.

**BOB:** *(voice-over)* I've been following Alice on Instagram for months. She just shared her Zeyaroo profile link in her bio, so let's give it a try.

**[Bob clicks "Send Inquiry" on Alice's profile]**

**[A form appears: project name, event date, budget range, message]**

**BOB:** I'll fill this in:
- **Project name:** "Bob & Tania's Wedding"
- **Event date:** December 14
- **Budget range:** LKR 150,000 – 200,000
- **Message:** "Hi Alice! Getting married in December at Mount Lavinia Hotel. Would love full-day coverage plus an engagement shoot. Are you available?"

**[Bob clicks "Send Inquiry"]**

**NARRATOR:** That's it. The inquiry creates a project shell on both sides — Alice gets a notification, Bob can track the conversation under his Inquiries tab.

---

## SCENE 2 — Alice Reviews & Sends a Proposal

**[ON SCREEN: Alice's dashboard. A new inquiry pulses in the sidebar]**

**ALICE:** *(reading)* "Bob & Tania's Wedding"… December 14… budget looks workable. Let me put together a proper proposal.

**[Alice opens the inquiry, clicks "Create Proposal"]**

**NARRATOR:** A proposal in Zeyaroo isn't just a number — it's broken into **milestones**. Each milestone is a chunk of work, with its own deliverables and its own agreed-upon amount. Up to ten per project.

**ALICE:** I'll structure this as three milestones:

| # | Milestone | Amount | Due |
|---|-----------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Engagement shoot + 20 edited photos | LKR 35,000 | Nov 5 |
| 2 | Wedding day full coverage | LKR 100,000 | Dec 14 |
| 3 | Final album — 200 edited photos + highlight video | LKR 45,000 | Jan 15 |

**Total: LKR 180,000**

**[Alice writes a short scope note for each milestone, attaches her standard contract PDF, hits "Send Proposal"]**

**NARRATOR:** Alice's proposal is now in **pending** state on Bob's side. He'll get an email and an in-app notification.

---

## SCENE 3 — Bob Negotiates (Counter-Proposal)

**[ON SCREEN: Bob's notification — "Alice sent you a proposal"]**

**BOB:** Looks great overall, but I'd like to swap the highlight video for a few extra prints, and bring the total down a bit.

**[Bob clicks "Counter-Propose" on milestone 3]**

**NARRATOR:** This is where Zeyaroo shines. Bob doesn't have to start a new email thread or rewrite the proposal. He edits in place, and Zeyaroo creates a **new immutable version** — version 2 — preserving Alice's original.

**BOB:** I'll change milestone 3 to:
- "Final album — 200 edited photos + 10 printed photos (8x10), no highlight video"
- Amount: LKR 35,000

**Total now: LKR 170,000.** And I'll add a note: "Hey Alice, prints matter more to my parents than a video. Hope this works!"

**[Bob clicks "Send Counter-Proposal"]**

**[ON SCREEN: A version history panel shows v1 (Alice's) and v2 (Bob's) side by side, with diffs highlighted]**

---

## SCENE 4 — Alice Reviews and Accepts

**ALICE:** Fair enough. Prints actually take less editing time than a highlight reel — I'm fine with this.

**[Alice opens v2, reviews the diff, clicks "Accept Proposal"]**

**NARRATOR:** The moment Alice accepts, the proposal moves to **finalized**, and the project transitions from `inquiry` to `active`. Both sides see a confirmation screen. The contract is locked in.

**[ON SCREEN: A green "Project Active" banner. The three milestones now appear, each ready to begin.]**

---

## SCENE 5 — Starting the First Milestone

**NARRATOR:** With the proposal finalized, Bob and Alice agree on payment for milestone 1 and Bob settles it through their preferred method. Once Alice confirms she's received it, the milestone moves into progress and work begins.

**BOB:** Sending payment for milestone 1 — LKR 35,000.

**ALICE:** *(notification ping)* Payment confirmed. Time to get to work.

**[ON SCREEN: Milestone 1 status flips to **in progress**]**

---

## SCENE 6 — Delivery (Milestone 1)

**[Time skip — engagement shoot done]**

**ALICE:** I shot the engagement session yesterday. Let me upload the gallery.

**[Alice opens the project, clicks "Upload Media", drags 20 edited JPEGs into the upload zone]**

**NARRATOR:** As Alice uploads, Zeyaroo automatically generates three variants of each photo — a thumbnail, a medium preview, and the original. **Crucially, while a milestone is still in progress, every preview Bob sees is watermarked.** The originals are protected.

**[ON SCREEN: Bob's view — 20 photos, each stamped with a faint diagonal "ZEYAROO PREVIEW" watermark]**

**ALICE:** *(adding a note in the milestone thread)* "Here's the engagement gallery! Let me know if any need re-edits before I close this milestone."

**[Alice clicks "Mark Milestone Ready for Review"]**

---

## SCENE 7 — Bob Approves & Unlocks the Gallery

**BOB:** *(scrolling through previews)* These are gorgeous. Tania's going to love that one by the lighthouse.

**[Bob clicks "Approve Milestone"]**

**[ON SCREEN: A confirmation modal — "This will mark the milestone complete and unlock the unwatermarked originals for download."]**

**BOB:** Confirm.

**NARRATOR:** Two things happen at once:
1. Watermarks are removed from milestone 1's photos for Bob.
2. Bob unlocks full-resolution downloads.

**[ON SCREEN: Bob's gallery now shows clean, watermark-free photos with a "Download All" button.]**

---

## SCENE 8 — The Wedding Day & Milestone 2

**[Time skip — December 14]**

**NARRATOR:** Wedding day. Bob already settled milestone 2 a week ago to lock Alice's calendar. Alice shoots the wedding, then over two weeks uploads the full set of photos.

**[ON SCREEN: Fast cut — Alice uploading 800+ raw selects, then a curated set of 400 edited shots. Bob browsing watermarked previews on his phone in bed.]**

**BOB:** This is everything I hoped for. Approving milestone 2.

**[Click → milestone complete → watermarks lifted on milestone 2 batch]**

---

## SCENE 9 — Albums, Shares & Family

**NARRATOR:** Zeyaroo isn't just for the client — it's for the people around the client too. Bob organizes the photos into albums and shares them with family.

**[Bob creates two albums: "Ceremony" and "Reception". He drags photos into each.]**

**[Bob clicks "Create Share Link" on the Ceremony album]**

**[A modal appears: optional password, optional expiration date]**

**BOB:** I'll password-protect this one — `tania2025` — and set it to expire in 30 days. Sending the link to my parents on WhatsApp.

**NARRATOR:** Shares are public by token. No Zeyaroo account required. Family clicks the link, types the password, browses the gallery — that's it.

---

## SCENE 10 — Final Milestone & Project Completion

**[Time skip — January]**

**ALICE:** Final album is done. 200 hand-edited photos, and the prints are at the lab — should be at Bob's address by Friday.

**[Alice uploads the final 200 edited photos, marks milestone 3 ready]**

**[Bob receives the prints, opens the gallery one more time]**

**BOB:** Approving the last milestone.

**[Click → milestone complete → project status: active → completed]**

**[ON SCREEN: A celebration banner — "Project Completed". Bob has a downloadable archive of all 620 photos at full resolution.]**

---

## SCENE 11 — Wrap-Up & Reviews

**NARRATOR:** Once a project is completed, both sides can leave a review on each other's profiles. Alice's review boosts her public creator profile; Bob's track record helps her trust future clients.

**ALICE:** *(typing)* "Bob and Tania were a dream to work with. Communication on Zeyaroo made everything painless."

**BOB:** *(typing)* "Alice delivered on every milestone, on time, exactly as promised."

---

## CLOSING — What You Just Saw

**NARRATOR:** *(over a recap montage)* That's Zeyaroo end-to-end:

1. **Inquiry** — Bob reaches out to Alice from her public profile.
2. **Proposal** — Alice breaks the work into milestones with prices and deliverables.
3. **Negotiation** — Bob counter-proposes; Zeyaroo keeps every version.
4. **Finalization** — Both sides agree, the project goes active.
5. **Milestone-by-milestone work** — payment is settled, work begins, watermarked previews go up.
6. **Approval** — Bob approves; watermarks lift, originals unlock.
7. **Sharing** — Bob organizes albums and shares with family via secure links.
8. **Completion** — Final milestone closes, project archives, both leave reviews.

**No chasing payments. No watermark leaks. No lost message threads. Just the work, the trust, and the delivery — in one place.**

---

### Production notes for recording
- Plan ~8–10 minutes total. Each scene 30–60 seconds.
- Use real screen recordings on a seeded dev environment — consider using `zeyaroo-admin` to pre-create Alice and Bob with realistic data.
- Edit in **side-by-side splits** during the proposal/negotiation scenes so viewers see both perspectives at once.
- Add captioned callouts whenever a status changes (e.g. "pending → in progress → complete") so viewers internalize the milestone flow without you having to narrate it.
