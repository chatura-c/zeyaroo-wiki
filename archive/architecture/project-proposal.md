Project Proposal: Creator-Client Media Collaboration Platform

1. Executive Summary

This platform is designed to streamline the professional relationship between media creators (photographers/videographers) and their clients. It provides a centralized ecosystem for inquiries, milestone-based project management, collaborative media storage, and secure escrowed payments.

A key differentiator of the platform is its Hybrid Storage Model, which incentivizes creators to commit to long-term reserved storage while offering clients flexible, pay-as-you-go dynamic storage.

2. Core Entity Definitions

2.1 Users & Profiles

Creator: Professionals who maintain public-facing profiles. The profile acts as a digital resume, showcasing portfolios, service categories, and past client reviews.

Client: Accounts (individuals or businesses) that hire creators. They primarily interact via project inquiries and media viewing.

2.2 Project Structure

Project: The top-level container for a physical event or engagement.

Ownership: Every project has a single owner.

Transferability: Owners can transfer the project to another account (e.g., from Creator to Client). This shifts the storage burden and data rights to the new owner.

Collaborations: Projects allow for "Collaborators," enabling multiple creators to contribute media objects to a single shared project event.

Albums: Logical collections within a project (e.g., "Wedding Ceremony," "Reception," "Behind the Scenes"). Every album must belong to exactly one project.

Media Objects: Individual photos or videos stored within albums.

Proposed Pricing Strategy:

Reserved: $0.02/GB/mo (purchased in 500GB or 1TB blocks).

Dynamic: $0.10/GB/mo (billed based on daily average usage).

Incentive: By making Reserved storage significantly cheaper ($20/TB vs $100/TB equivalent), creators are encouraged to maintain a professional subscription, while clients pay a small premium for the convenience of not managing a subscription.

4. The Proposal & Inquiry Workflow

4.1 Inquiry Phase

Clients initiate a project by visiting a Creator profile and submitting an inquiry including:

Event Date & Location

Deliverable Requirements (e.g., "I need 50 edited photos")

Timeline Expectations

Budget Range

4.2 Negotiation & Finalization

The Creator responds with a Formal Proposal through the platform:

Drafting: Creator sets milestones, prices, and specific deliverable counts.

Iterative Edits: The client can suggest changes (e.g., "Add a 30-second highlight reel"). Each edit is tracked as a version.

Finalization: Both parties digitally sign the proposal.

Initial Invoice: An automated invoice is generated. The project is "Active" once the payment for the first milestone is deposited.

5. Milestone & Escrow System

To ensure trust, the platform uses a Milestone-Based Escrow.

5.1 Milestone Characteristics

Quantifiable Tasks: Each milestone must be measurable (e.g., "Upload 20 item photos").

Payment Association: Each milestone has a specific dollar value.

Maximum Cap: To prevent fragmentation, projects are limited to 10 milestones.

5.2 The Payment Loop

Funding: Client deposits funds for the next milestone into the platform escrow.

Creation: Creator completes the task and uploads media to the Project Album.

Hold State: The media is visible as "Watermarked" or "Low-Res" (or inaccessible) until the payment is released.

Release: Once the client approves the milestone or the deadline passes without dispute, funds are released to the Creator.

5.3 Contingency Handling

Non-Payment Protection: If the Creator completes Milestone N, but the Client hasn't funded Milestone N+1, the Creator's deadlines for future milestones are automatically extended by the duration of the delay.

Final Milestone: Every project concludes with a "Project Completion" milestone which triggers the final transfer of full-resolution assets.

6. Project Ownership Transfer

Post-delivery, the Creator can initiate an Ownership Transfer:

Benefit to Creator: Frees up their "Reserved Storage" quota.

Benefit to Client: Gives them permanent control over the assets.

Action: Once the Client accepts, the storage costs are calculated against the Client’s "Dynamic Storage" balance or their own "Reserved" plan.

7. Delivery Timelines & Relatables

When an inquiry is made, the following "Relatables" must be defined in the proposal:

RAW vs. Edited: Specify if the client receives unedited footage.

Usage Rights: Commercial vs. Personal use licenses.

Equipment Clause: Specific gear required for the project.

Revision Rounds: Number of times a client can request edits on a delivered media object.
